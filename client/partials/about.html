My name is Lucy...

I am a runner. I am not crazy. I am not insane. I am a runner. When I was in fifth grade, I decided I was going to join the track team. Track sounds fun, right? I thought so, at the time.

But, I never thought I would become a distance runner. Looking back now, choosing distance running was one of the best decisions I have ever made. Running cross country and track has taught me so many important life lessons. Being a distance runner can put a lot of stress on your body.

I know this from experience. Whether it's shin pain, side cramps, or the occasional throwing up, I still get up, decide to lace up, and continue putting in the miles every single day. There are times that I complain about how I don't want to go to practice because I have work to do, or I would rather nap, but then I remember that I have a team and coaches that are counting on me.
